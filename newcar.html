Triumph Spitfire

The original Spitfire, a single-seater fighter plane used by the Royal Air Force and its allies, made a name for itself as a saviour of the U.K. during the Second World War. The bubbly two-seat sports car that was the Triumph Spitfire (1962-1980) wasn’t nearly as lofty in looks or actions, but it grew into a classic all the same. Interestingly, in the 1960s, Vickers/Supermarine, which built the plane, wanted to use the name Vanguard for another plane. Triumph, which built the car, owned the rights, so they allowed the use of Vanguard if they could use the name Spitfire for its next project.
